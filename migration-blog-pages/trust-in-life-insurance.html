What is a Trust in Life Insurance? Complete Guide

Learn what a trust in life insurance is, how it works, and how it provides tax savings, faster claim payouts, and financial protection for beneficiaries.

What is a Trust?

A trust is a legal arrangement where one person (the settler) gives control of their assets to another person or entity (the trustee) for the benefit of a third person (the beneficiary).

In life insurance, a trust can be used to manage how the death benefit is distributed to your loved ones.

Setting up a trust can provide several advantages, including protecting the payout from creditors, ensuring it is used for specific purposes (like education), and potentially reducing inheritance tax liabilities.

Key Takeaways

  • Trusts help in managing the distribution of life insurance proceeds.
  • They provide legal protection and tax benefits.
  • Beneficiaries receive the funds according to the rules set by the settler.
